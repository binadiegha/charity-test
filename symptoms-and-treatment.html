Symptoms

Some common symptoms of fibroids may include

  • Heavy Menstrual Bleeding (HMB)

  • Blood Clots

  • Anemia

  • Abdominal Cramping

  • Pelvic Pain

  • Back Pain

  • Leg Pain

  • Bulk Symptoms

  • Pain with sexual intercourse (known as dyspareunia)

Treatment

Treatment options for fibroids vary and should be personalized based on factors such as symptoms, future pregnancy plans, fibroid size and location, age, and proximity to menopause. Consult with your doctor to discuss the most suitable treatment approach tailored to your specific situation. If you have fibroids without symptoms, regular check-ups during routine exams will help monitor their growth.

Treating fibroid symptoms involves various options:

Birth Control

Low-dose pills and Mirena® IUD, containing progesterone-like medication, can manage heavy bleeding without promoting fibroid growth.

Pain Medication

Over-the-counter NSAIDs like ibuprofen or naproxen may relieve mild pain and reduce heavy menstrual bleeding associated with fibroids.

Endometrial Ablation:

A minor surgical procedure that removes or destroys the uterine lining using various methods like laser, wire loops, or microwaves. This helps control very heavy bleeding and can be done on an outpatient basis or in a doctor's office.

There are two main surgical approaches for fibroid removal

Hysterectomy

A major surgical procedure involving the complete removal of the uterus. This definitive solution stops menstrual bleeding, relieves pelvic pressure, and prevents the growth of new fibroids. However, pregnancy becomes impossible after a hysterectomy.

Myomectomy

This surgery removes fibroids while preserving the healthy tissue of the uterus. Myomectomy can be conducted through major abdominal surgery, laparoscopy, or hysteroscopy, depending on the fibroids' type, size, and location.

Fibroids can be diagnosed using various tests such as

Ultrasound

  • Uses soundwaves to create images of the uterus and pelvic organs.
  • It may not always provide a complete picture; more detailed tests like MRI and hysteroscopy are recommended.

Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)

  • Provides detailed information on fibroid size, location, and blood supply.
  • Essential for surgical planning due to its comprehensive imaging

Hysteroscopy

  • Involves a slender device to visualize the inside of the uterus.
  • Allows healthcare professionals to identify fibroids within the uterine cavity.

Lab Tests

  • Recommended if experiencing abnormal menstrual bleeding.
  • Includes a complete blood count (CBC) to check for anemia and other blood tests for potential causes..
