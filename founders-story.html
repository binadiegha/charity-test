Founder's Story

Favour Nwabueze

Favour Nwabueze’s journey to founding Thrive Beyond Fibroids is deeply personal, as she got diagnosed with Fibroids at the young age of 21. Her diagnosis fueled her inquisition as she mirrored the reality of a smaller percentage of young women diagnosed at an early age who are outliers. The severity of her symptoms from her diagnosis was excruciating abdominal pain, joint pain, anemia, and heavy menstruation, and this had a drastic impact on her career productivity and caused social reclusivity. In Favour's home country of Nigeria, there is an extreme lack of open dialogue regarding fibroids, misinformation, societal stigma, a lack of affordable invasive and non-invasive procedures, and the never-ending “Go Get Married to Cure Fibroids” mentality in Africa.

Favour’s curiosity and Myomectomy Journey ignited the creation of Thrive Beyond Fibroids after hearing the stories of other women experiencing similar pain, and she set out to bridge the gap in women’s health and crucially limit cases like hers by fostering a global community that contributes drastically to the reduction in the statistics of women living with Fibroids in Africa with access to reliable information, preventive measures through lifestyle changes and dietary changes, Patient advocacy and information on how to thrive despite this diagnosis.
